Faction Descriptions

More lore to come soon! In the meantime... Speculation ;)

Children of The Sun

Homeworld: Earth

A Religious Theocratic Monarchy based on revering the sun as a savior or divine being. In turn all Infused are treated as blessed by the sun. Infused serve as the generals, nobility, and other high-ranking officials. There is a large gap between them and the lower class mundane peoples.

The First Cycle

Homeworld: Venus

Following The Event, the First Cycle sought out to discover the inner workings of what happened exactly and how the Infused and Solar Minerals have come about exactly. They have set up their capital center on Venus to be closer to the sun. The First Cycle is a Tech-Driven prosperous nation that looks down on the others and see them as uncivilized and often undeserving of the knowledge they have gained.

Old World Coalition

Homeworld: Mars

The Old World Coalition was born out of the earliest colonies on Mars, The Old World Coalition established independance only a few decades before The Event. As the original earth population slid into what the Martians saw as theocratic insanity, the Old World Coalition sought to preserve what remained of the cultures of modern earth. In time, they offically declared sovereignty over Mars and fought a hard revolutionary war with the newly cristined Children of the Sun. The Patriotic pride of the OWC is unmatched, and they highly value personal freedom of choice and expression.

Gas Giant Collective

Homeworld: Saturn (Strong Jupiter Presence)

The Gas Giant Collective was originally the collection of earth's far flung mining operations. After the rise of the Children of the Sun, Infused and Solar Minerals made the mining stations increasingly obsolete and caused them to be abandoned. Far from the sun, the people of the GGC struggle to scrape out traditional lives mostly void of Solar influence.

Faction Rules

Children of The Sun

Solar Miracles: At the beginning of each turn, roll 3d3. The total result of these dice is your total number of "Miracle Points" available this turn. Your Miracle Points reset at the beginning of the next turn. Points from the Miracle Pool may be spent to:

  • Increase a Ship's Thrust during the Order Phase (1 point per +2 Thrust)
  • Increase the To-Hit number during the "Roll to Hit" step for all attacks during one firing of a Direct Fire Weapon from an enemy ship (2 points per +1 to Target Number)
  • Decrease the target number for the Accuracy Roll of a single missile (2 points per -1 to Accuracy)
  • Increase the AP value of a Weapon until the end of the turn (1 Point per +1 AP)
  • Restore one System on one Ship (4 Points per 1 System)

Faction Upgrade Name Rules Text
Holy Sanctuary

When this ship is targeted by weapons on opponent's ship during the "Declare Targets" step, for each weapon targeting this ship, roll 1d6. For each 6+, the shots assigned to this ship for that weapon must target another ship. If there are no more valid targets, the attacks are lost. (If a ship has more than one instance of holy sanctuary, roll for each)
Forced Reverance

At the beginning of the turn, after thrust orders are given but before thrust orders are applied, all opponent's ships within 12" of this ship have their velocity reduced by half rounded up.
Resplendent Return

If this ship is destroyed, roll 1d6. On a 4+, return this ship to the battlefield at the beginning of the next turn. Place it anywhere within your deployment zone, with 6 Velocity, facing any direction, with all IP and Systems restored, then destroy all instances of Resplendent Return on this ship.

The First Cycle

Slipspace Deployment: At the beginning of the game, choose any number of ships with total integrity equal to or less than 1/3 of the total integrity of your force. At the beginning of the game, instead of deploying those ships in your deployment zone, keep them off the field. At the beginning of any turn except the first and second turns, place any number of those ships anywhere on the battlefield at least 12" from any enemy ships and any objective markers, at 6 velocity, facing any direction. Those ships may not be given movement orders the turn they are deployed.

Faction Upgrade Name Rules Text
Harmonic Drive

During the "Order Phase", you may also assign any amount of thrust to the Harmonic Drives equipped on this ship.

At the beginning of the "Movement Phase", remove this ship from the field. Then place it anywhere on the field up to a number of inches away from the point it was removed equal to the amount of thrust assigned to the Harmonic Drives multiplied by the number of intact Harmonic Drives equipped on this ship, and facing it's previous heading.

For example, a Frigate-Class ship 2 Harmonic Drive upgrades assigns 6 thrust to the Harmonic Drives. Before it moves according to its velocity and course change, it may be removed from the field and replaced up to 12" away from its position at the beginning of the turn, and facing the heading it started the turn with
Binding Runes

Assigned to a weapon when mounted.

Direct Fire Weapons assigned to Binding Runes may measure their range bands (if a direct fire weapon) or measure minimum ranges (if a missile weapon) as if they were mounted on another ship, that ship must also have a weapon mounted that's assigned to Binding Runes.
Chameleon Core

At the beginning of the turn, choose an upgrade mounted on this ship. This upgrade provides the benefits of the chosen upgrade, in addition to having this ability, until it gets changed this way again.

If the upgrade this upgrade is changed to requires pre-game actions to function as intended (ex. assigning to a weapon) perform those actions when this upgrade changes.

Haywire Missiles

Inertial Weaponry

After the "Deal Damage" step when firing a weapon from this ship, reduce or increase the velocity of each targeted ship by up to half (rounded up) of the total damage dealt to that ship.

Gas Giant Collective

Rugged Construction: Missiles fired from Gas Giant Collective ships have +1 Durability. Additionally, when a Gas Giant Collective ship takes damage, for each point of damage taken, roll 1d6. On a result of 6+, ignore that damage (This roll is made in addition to the roll from the "Adaptive Plating" upgrade if that upgrade is equipped)

Faction Upgrade Name Rules Text
Additional Fail-safes

This upgrade can be chosen to be "destroyed" up to 3 times.
Kineticite Plating

When this ship takes damage, note the amount of damage taken. During the next Order Phase, this ship has additional thrust equal to the number noted. Reset the number noted to zero.
Imposing Presence

If this ship and an enemy ship both would capture an objective, instead of neither ship capturing the point, the point comes under the control of the ship with the higher number of Imposing Presence upgrades mounted.
