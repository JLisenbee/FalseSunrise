Term Definitions

  • Component: A blanket term for all the Weapons and Upgrades on a ship.
  • Die Rolls (YdX, dX, and X+): When rolling dice, these short hands are commonly used throughout the rules of False Sunrise:
    • YdX and dX: This shorthand means "Roll Y dice each with X sides and sum the results." In the case of "dX", just roll one die with X sides.
    • X+: This shorthand means "Roll a six-sided die. if the result is greater than or equal to X, the roll is a success."
  • Fleet: A player's total force, comprised of all ships controlled by them.
  • Point Defense: Specific designation on some Upgrades that communicate that they are meant to engage missiles (and possibly eventually Small Craft.)
  • Ship: Spacefaring vessel, making up a player's fleet. Has Systems, Integrity, and Thrust, all of which are recorded on the Ship Sheet. Ship's can be represented by anything that has a clear center and a clear "forward" direction. Models/game pieces for ships do not matter, as ship's in False Sunrise are always only considered to be the centerpoints of the gamepieces used.
  • System: A blanket term for all Weapons and Upgrades on a ship, as well as some "built in" things including Engines, Fire Control, and the Command Bridge.
  • Thrust: A ship's ability to alter it's velocity and direction.
  • Upgrade: Non-offensive modules installed on a ship, a list can be found under their own section below.
  • Velocity: A ship's actual current speed.
  • Weapon: Any directly offensive parts of the ship that deal damage, strictly Direct Fire and Missile weapons (does not include Point Defenses).

Pregame Setup

  • 4' x 6' play area with ~7-10 Pieces of terrain. Terrain is impassable and blocks line of sight.
  • Players roll a die. This is the initiative roll. The player that won the initive roll has the initiative.
  • The player who has the initiative places an objective marker anywhere on the play area 12" from any table edge and not on any terrain.
  • Players alternate placing objective markers in this manner, with each one also at least 9" from each other, until 5 objective markers are placed.
  • The person who has the initiative chooses a board edge and places all of their ships within 12" of that edge. Then the player does not places all their ships in the same manner on the opposite edge.
  • Play begins. Ships start at 6 Thrust.

Turn Structure

  • Order Phase: Players secretly note their movement orders (rules for this is found in "Movement" below). Players then simultaneously reveal those orders. Since there is no ship-to-ship collision in False Sunrise, movement may be executed simultaneously.
  • Movement Phase: For each ship, move it in accordance to the rules in "Movement" below. Since there is no ship-to-ship collision in False Sunrise, movement may be executed simultaneously.
  • Weapons Phase: The player who has the initiative chooses one ship to fire any or all of it's weapons. Then the player who does not chooses a ship to do the same. Alternate back and forth until all ships have fired.
  • Points Phase: Calculate points earned for this turn. (Refer to "Winning" below)
  • End Phase: Initiative transfers to the player who did not have it at the beginning of the turn, the turn ends, and the next turn begins.

Movement

  • Ships have a thrust value representing the power of their engines. This is not the actual distance a ship will move in a turn, but rather the "currency" they can spend to change the amount.
  • Accelerating: Up to all of a ship's thrust can go towards increasing it's velocity. A ship is not required to accelerate or decelerate, and instead choose to maintain it's current velocity.
  • Changing course: Up to half of a ship's thrust can be used to make course changes. Courses changes are made either to Port (left) or Starboard (right) and each point of course change changes the couyrse of the ship by one "hour" distance as if referencing a clock face (See included Tool). Course changes are always all port or all starboard, and cannot be split between the two.
  • Executing Movement: Movement is carried out following the steps below.
    1. Rotate the ship a number of "hour spaces" equal to half it's course change this turn, rounded down.
    2. Move the ship forward a number of inches equal to half it's velocity rounded up.
    3. Rotate the ship a number of "hour spaces" equal to half it's course change this turn, rounded up.
    4. Move the ship forward a number of inches equal to half it's velocity rounded down.
  • If a ship's path when moving would intersect a piece of terrain it immediately stops, then it takes damage equal to it's current velocity, then it's velocity is reduced to zero.
  • If a ship's movement would move it off the field remove the ship from the board. Note the point where it left. At the end of the next turn, reset it's velocity to 6, place it anywhere within 3" of the point it left the board, and face it any heading.

Winning

  • When a ship moves within 3" of the center of an objective marker, that marker is now controlled by them.
  • If more than one player moved within 3" of an objective marker during a single movement phase, that point remains in the state it was in at the beginning of the turn. (It remains under the control of whoever controlled it, or remains neutral if no one controlled it.)
  • At the end of each round (after all ships have had a chance to shoot), each player earns one point for each marker controlled by them.
  • Once a player has earned 15 points, the game ends and that player wins.

Force Construction

  • Choosing a Total Integrity: When constructing a force, first decide on a maximum amount of integrity points each team's fleet should total up to.
  • Ship hulls have an associated integrity value based on their relative size and capability, and equal integrity per fleet insures roughly equal sides (A good starting point is 20)
  • Choosing Hulls: Ship hulls have a number of slots for components (weapons and upgrades) equal to their integrity value. Players choose the hulls they wish to use up to the agreed upon Total Integrity.
  • Mounting Components: For each ship hull, you choose a number of components (weapons or upgrades) from the lists below equal to the Ship hull's integrity value. Any limitations or special rules for mounting a component will be noted in the descripion of that component.

Ships and Hulls

  • Ships have a number of slots for components equal to their integrity value.
  • Ships have a number of Integrity Rows, 6 pips each, equal to their integrity value.
  • When a ship takes damage, mark off pips one row at a time in one row equal to the damage dealt. All damage is dealt in that row until that row is depleted.
  • When a row is depleted, the ship's controller chooses one System (list below) on the ship to be destroyed.
  • When all rows are depleted, the ship is destroyed and removed from the field.
  • List of Systems:
    • Each Component is a System. When a Component is destroyed, it's effect no longer applies if it's an upgrade, or it cannot be fired if it's a weapon.
    • Engines: While a ship's Engines are destroyed, this ship's thrust is reduced to half it's original value.
    • Fire Control: While a ship's Fire Control is destroyed, subtract 3 from the die roll when shooting direct fire weapons on this ship, and subtract 2 from the accuracy rolls when firing missile weapons from this ship.
    • Control Bridge: While a ship's Control Bridge is destroyed, If this ship increases or decreases velocity or changes course during the movement phase, it may not also fire weapons during the weapons phase.

Direct Fire Weapons

  • Direct fire weapons fire out of the fore arc, the port (left) arc, the starboard (right) arc, or the rear arc.This is chosen during force creation.
    • The easiest way to define arcs is imagining the ship is at the center of a clock face, and thinking of the arcs in reference to the "hours" on the clock. Using the course change tool is helpful for this, it even has the arcs marked out.
    • Fore Arc: Spans from between the center between 10 and 11 o'clock, to the center between 1 and 2 o'clock.
    • Port Arc: Spans from between the center between 7 and 8 o'clock, to the center between 11 and 12 o'clock.
    • Starboard Arc: Spans from between the center between 1 and 2 o'clock, to the center between 4 and 5 o'clock.
    • Rear Arc: Spans from between the center between 7 and 8 o'clock, to the center between 4 and 5 o'clock.
  • To Fire Direct Fire weapons, follow these steps in order:
    1. Choose Weapons: Choose any number of direct fire weapons mounted on the ship to fire.
    2. Declare Targets: For each weapon chosen to fire, choose a number of targets up to the weapon's Attacks characteristic that are within the weapon's stated firing arc (or arcs in the case of a turret upgrade) and within line of sight.
    3. Determine Range: For each target, divide it's distance in inches from the attacking ship by the range characteristic of the weapon being fired and round up. The result is the Range Band that the target is within.
    4. Determine To-Hit value: In Range Band 1, direct fire weapons hit on 2+. For each subsequent range band, the To-Hit number increases by 1. (3+ for Range Band 2, 4+ For Range Band 3, etc.)
    5. Roll To Hit: For each weapon being fired, for each Attack, roll to see if it hits the target using the To-Hit value calculated previously.
    6. Deal damage: For each Attack that hit it's target, deal an amount of damage to that target equal to the Damage value of the weapon that made the attack.
    7. Roll for Additional Critical Hits: If the target was a ship, after applying damage, check the number of pips remaining in the target's lowest non-empty integrity bar. If the number of pips remaining is less than or equal to the Armor Piercing value of the weapon that dealt the damage, roll a d6. On a 4+, destroy an additional system on the target ship.

Missile Weapons

  • Missiles can be fired in all directions.
  • Missiles have a minimum range, rather than a maximum range. They can be fired infinitely far (details in steps below).
  • To Fire Missile weapons, follow these steps in order:
    1. Choose Weapons: Choose any number of missile weapons mounted on the ship to fire.
    2. Declare Targets: For each weapon chosen to fire, choose a number of targets up to the weapon's Attacks characteristic that are within line of sight.
    3. Determine Range: For each target, measure the distance to it from the attacking ship. If the target is further away from the attacking ship than the range characteristic of the weapon being fired, that target is within range.
    4. Roll To Hit: For each weapon being fired, for each Attack, roll to see if it hits the target using the Accuracty characteristic of the weapon being fired.
    5. Calculate PD Pool: If any missiles hit during the previous step, your opponent rolls all dice under the Damage characteristic for all Point Defense upgrades on the target ship, and all ships they control who have Point Defense weapons with a range that overlaps the target ship. This is the PD Pool.
    6. Engage Point Defenses: For each missile that would hit the target from this weapon, subtract their Durability value from the PD pool, one missile at a time, until ther are no more missiles to subtract from the pool (all missiles destroyed), or the PD Pool has insufficent points to subtract another missile (at least one missile made it through).
    7. Deal damage: If at least one missile made it through, those missiles deal damage to the target equal to their damage value.

Upgrades

  • Adaptive Plating

    • When this ship takes damage, for each point of damage taken, roll a d6. Ignore one point of damage for each 5+ on those dice.

  • Cloaking System

    • When this ship is being targetted by an attack from a Direct Fire weapon, the attacker gets -X to the roll when rolling to hit where X is the Range Band number this ship is in for that weapon minus 1. (-0 at RB 1, -1 at RB 2, -2 and RB 3, etc.)
    • When this ship is being targetted by a missile attack, this ship is counted as being 8" closer for the purposes of determing if it is in range.

  • Dazzler

    • Point Defense
    • Damage: 2d3
    • Range: 4"

  • Prophet PD

    • Point Defense
    • Damage: 1d3
    • Range: 9"

  • Reinforced Armor

    • Each Integrity bar for this ship has an additional 2 pips.

  • Repair Crews

    • At the end of this ship's activation, restore d3 integrity to this ship.
    • If this repair fills an integrity bar, also restore one system.

  • Spitfire CIWS

    • Point Defense
    • Damage: 3d3
    • Range: 1"

  • Target Acquisition Gear

    • At the beginning of the Weapons Phase, choose a ship within line of sight of this ship.
    • That ship can have missile weapons fired at it without requiring line of sight until the end of the Weapons Phase of the next turn.

  • Thruster Overchargers

    • You may increase your available thrust on an activation to up to twice it's original value.
    • If you do, Reduce your available thrust next activation by the amount you increased it by.

  • Tuned Fire Control

    • +1 to hit with all direct fire weapons. (Weapons fired within RB 1 are guaranteed hits)

  • Turret Mount

    • Must be assigned to a weapon when mounted.
    • Allows assigned weapon to fire in the two arcs adjacent to it's firing arc, in addition to it's assigned arc
